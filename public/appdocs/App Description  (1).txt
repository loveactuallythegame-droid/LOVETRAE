“Love, Actually… The Game
How about we DON’T Break up?” App Description
App Overview
"Love, Actually… The Game: How About We DON'T Break Up?" is a first-of-its-kind mobile-first, gamified couples therapy app and mobile game designed to help emotionally exhausted couples rebuild trust, overcome betrayal, communication, and intimacy after betrayal and fall in love again. It aims to transform couples into stronger, emotionally intelligent teams through playful, competitive therapy sessions —hosted in real-time by an AI host, Dr. Marcie Liss (Merciless) — a witty, sarcastic, and brutally honest AI-powered guide who leads couples through daily games, quizzes, and challenges.
App Purpose
The core purpose is to deliver brutally honest therapy disguised as fun, using game mechanics to foster growth and repair relationships by transforming the difficult healing process into an engaging, fun competition — turning tough conversations into a game you can win together.
Technical Architecture & Tech Stack
* Frontend: React (or preferred JS framework), mobile-responsive with dark mode.
* Backend: Node.js with Express or similar.
* Database: Supabase (for authentication, profile, and data storage).
* AI Integration: GPT-4o or Anthropic Claude for therapy dialogue and fight analysis.
* Voice Synthesis: ElevenLabs style AI voice for the animated avatar.
* Real-time Features: WebSocket or Firebase for live scoring and notifications.
* Payment: Stripe integration for freemium + subscription.
* UI Design: Consistent pink and purple branding with playful animations and subtle haptic feedback for key actions.
Core Components & Features
Couple Linking System
* Secure login and partner invite system to sync profiles.
* Tracks joint and individual progress once linked.
Detailed Onboarding & Consent
During registration, players go through a comprehensive onboarding process. This is where they give consent for the Consequence Engine and provide their backstory and personal context.
* Origin Story Input ("Tell Auntie Marcie the Tea"): Before linking, each user provides a "Relationship Origin Debrief" detailing how they met, what they loved, when things broke, biggest betrayals, and healing efforts. This gives Dr. Marcie full emotional context for personalized interactions.
* Emotional X-Ray Questionnaire: Follow-up questions are tailored based on initial responses to reveal deeper psychological needs and conflict responses.
Daily Therapy Duels (Gamified Challenges)
* A library of 200+ daily, 10 to 30 minute challenges designed to rebuild trust and intimacy.
* Challenges come in three formats: Emotional Connection (SEEN Method), Psych-based Games (Gottman-inspired), and Creative Chaos (role-play, photos, reenactments).
* Dr. Marcie introduces, times, and sassily grades each task.
* Each game is a mobile-responsive module with a pink and purple interface, featuring Dr. Marcie’s avatar, interactive buttons, timers, scoreboards, and playful animations.
* AI (GPT-4o or Anthropic Claude) generates personalized prompts, analyzes inputs, delivers feedback (sarcastic but supportive), and adjusts difficulty/tone based on user history.
* Examples of games include "Truth or Trust," "Emotional Charades," "Love Language Showdown," and "Slap of Truth."
Consequence Engine
* Enforces "real-world penalties" for skipped or underperformed tasks.
* Examples include changing the user’s phone screensaver to humorous images, temporarily blocking distracting apps, setting recurring alarms.
Live Scoring & Game Mechanics
* Real-time scoring dashboard (similar to Quizizz) tracking "Vulnerability Points," "Trust Thermometer," "Streaks," and "Breakthroughs."
* Partner-to-partner comparison on dashboards.
* "Redemption Rounds" to win back points.
* Awards points, badges, or unlockables based on performance.
Quizzes, Trivia, & Redemption Questions
* Psychology-based quizzes on topics like attachment styles, conflict styles, and love languages.
* Marcie delivers custom feedback based on answers.
SOS Fight Solver Function
* An emergency "FIX THIS MESS" button for partners to press during conflicts.
* Partners are separated into private "writing booths" to submit their side of the issue.
* AI analyzes both inputs, compares perspectives, provides reasons for each partner's feelings, identifies who is right/wrong, who should apologize, explains why, and suggests how to move forward.
* A tailored healing challenge is assigned to improve the specific problematic area.
Dr. Marcie Liss: The AI Host
Dr. Marcie Liss is the witty AI therapist who guides users through the app.
* Animated Avatar: Sassy, stylish, and expressive, described as a cross between Jessica Rabbit, Wednesday Addams, and a hot therapist. Her aesthetic is "Classy 1950s noir meets modern glam" with a soft pastel blouse, hair up, biting red lipstick, pearl necklace, black gloves, and a digital clipboard of truths. Her expressions include smirks, side-eyes, raised brows, and a tight-lipped smile of judgment.
* Voice Integration: Sarcastic, sweet-but-venomous tone using ElevenLabs-style AI voice synthesis.
* Role: She introduces games, guides players, delivers prompts, quizzes, personalized real-time feedback, witty commentary, and enforces consequences.
* Personality & Dialogue Examples: She is brutally honest, emotionally intelligent, and possesses a sharp wit. She uses humor and sarcasm to deliver tough truths and motivate users.
